Historical Places in India
pride of the nation

The many temples, forts, palaces and monuments in the country make up the historical places of India. These reflect the splendid heritage and culture of the country. The historical places in India depict the stunning craftsmanship on the stone which can be seen in many temples and forts.

1. Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the seven wonders of the world and is considered to be the most popular tourist attraction in the country.

2. Qutub Minar

At a height of 234 feet, this minaret is the tallest individual tower in the world and is a perfect display of Mughal architectural brilliance.

3. Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal stands at the intersection of the main road in Jaipur, Badi Chaupad. Signature building of Jaipur and was built by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.

4. Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building, which is considered to be the pride of Kolkata. Built between 1906 and 1921, it is dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria.

5. India Gate

India Gate, is a war memorial located in New Delhi. It is dedicated to the 82,000 soldiers, both Indian and British, who died during the First World War and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.

6. Jallianwala Bagh

Jallianwala Bagh is a place of great historical importance during the Indian struggle for Independence. It is a public garden in Amritsar which houses a memorial of national importance.

7. Sun Temple

It is believed that the temple was constructed by King Narasimhadeva I of Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site

8. Red Fort

The Red Fort was the official seat of Mughal rule, when Shah Jahan decided to move the capital of the empire from Agra to Delhi.
